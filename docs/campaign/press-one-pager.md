# PRESS ONE-PAGER

## Barcelona Endorsed the UN's Open Source Principles. New York — Which *Hosts* the UN's Open Source Movement — Still Hasn't.

**A campaign by WeGovNYC / Sarapis calling on NYC to become the first city in the Americas to endorse the UN Open Source Principles.**

---

### The story in one sentence
The United Nations gathers the world's open source community in New York City
every June — yet a European city that merely *attended* that gathering, Barcelona,
has already done what New York has not: formally committed to the UN's standard for
building technology as a public good.

### Why now
- **June 2026 — UN Open Source Week**, UN Headquarters, NYC: 2,600+ participants
  from 120+ countries.
- **November 2025 — Barcelona** becomes the **first city in the world** to
  formally endorse the **UN Open Source Principles**, sparked by attending UN Open
  Source Week.
- **March 2025 —** seventeen organizations endorse the Principles — first the
  **Open Source Initiative**, then sixteen more including the **Linux Foundation
  and the Eclipse Foundation**.
- **2026 —** New York City, the permanent host of this global forum, has **still
  not endorsed** them.

### The "Barcelona beat NYC" angle
Barcelona's endorsement was not a costly IT overhaul — it was a **signature plus a
roadmap**: a citizen agreement on democratic technologies, an Open Source
Programme Office modeled on Paris and Munich, and a municipal open source fund. If
a visiting city can commit, the host city can lead.

### Why New York is the obvious next signatory
- **First U.S. city** to submit a **Voluntary Local Review** to the UN (2018).
- **First U.S. city** to join the UN **Safe Cities Global Initiative**.
- **Open Data Law since 2012**; municipal code already on **GitHub**.
- An **Office of Technology & Innovation** ready to host an **NYC OSPO**.

### The ask
WeGovNYC calls on the **Mayor's Office of Technology & Innovation** and the
**Mayor's Office for International Affairs** to **formally endorse the UN Open
Source Principles — making New York the first city in the Americas to do so** —
and to pair it with a light-touch roadmap beginning with an NYC Open Source
Programme Office.

### The eight principles (in brief)
Open by default · Contribute back · Secure by design · Foster inclusion · Design
for reusability · Provide documentation · RISE (recognize, incentivize, support,
empower) · Sustain and scale.
